🎯 Chess Tactics & Pawn Concepts

♞ Fork

One piece attacks two or more enemy pieces at once — usually a knight.

🏹 Skewer

An attack that hits a valuable piece first, then a weaker one behind it.

🔎 Discovered Attack

Moving one piece reveals an attack from another behind it.

🐴 Smothered Mate

Checkmate by a knight when the king is trapped by its own pieces.

💣 Desperado

A piece about to be lost sacrifices itself for max value.

♟ Passed Pawn

A pawn with no enemies blocking its promotion path.

♟ Doubled Pawns

Two friendly pawns stacked on the same file — usually a weakness.

♟ Isolated Pawn

No friendly pawns on adjacent files to help defend.

➡ Flank Pawn

Pawns on the edge (a, b, g, h-files), useful in side attacks.

⛓ Pawn Chain

Pawns supporting each other diagonally, forming a solid structure.

😵 Zugzwang

A situation where any move worsens your position — often decisive in endgames.

🔁 Perpetual Check

Endless checks that can’t be escaped — forces a draw.

🎓 Openings with Video Lessons

Najdorf Sicilian

A sharp and aggressive response to 1.e4, used by Fischer and Kasparov.

London System

A simple and solid setup for White, great against many defenses.

Nimzo-Indian Defense

Black’s flexible reply to 1.d4, targeting the center early on.

Ruy Lopez

A classical opening that builds pressure on the center and e5 pawn.

English Opening

Controls the center from the side — flexible and strategic.

Pirc Defense

Black allows White space and waits to counterattack.

Fried Liver Attack

A wild and tactical Italian line — deadly if opponent slips.

King's Gambit

White sacrifices a pawn early to open lines and attack fast.

Caro-Kann Defense

A rock-solid defense against 1.e4 that focuses on structure.

Queen's Gambit

White offers a pawn for center control and quick development.
