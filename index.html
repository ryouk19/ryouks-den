Fork

A fork is when one piece (often a knight) attacks two or more enemy pieces at the same time. The opponent can only save one, so you gain material.

Skewer

A skewer is a tactic where a valuable piece is attacked first, and when it moves, a weaker piece behind it gets captured. Itâ€™s the reverse of a pin.

Discovered Attack

When one piece moves away, uncovering an attack from another piece. Can be deadly when it also involves a check (discovered check).

Smothered Mate

A checkmate delivered by a knight when the king is surrounded ("smothered") by its own pieces and has no escape.

Desperado

A tactic where a piece that's going to be lost anyway makes a final sacrifice to gain material before it dies.

Najdorf Sicilian

A sharp and aggressive response to 1.e4, used by top players like Fischer and Kasparov.

London System

A simple yet solid setup for White, easy to learn and powerful against many defenses.

Nimzo-Indian Defense

A flexible and strategic response to 1.d4, aiming to challenge the center early.

Ruy Lopez

A classical and deep opening that targets the e5 pawn and builds long-term pressure.

English Opening

A flexible opening that focuses on slow central control through the flanks.

Pirc Defense

A defense that allows White to take space while Black prepares counterattacks.

Fried Liver Attack

A wild and tactical weapon in the Italian Game, ideal for catching opponents off guard early.

King's Gambit

An aggressive choice by White, offering a pawn to dominate the center and open lines fast.

Caro-Kann Defense

One of the most solid defenses against 1.e4, focusing on structure and development.

Queen's Gambit

A classical opening where White gives up a pawn temporarily to control the center and develop quickly.
